Microsoft: “Edge is 9% faster now, so please stop downloading Chrome with it”

In a groundbreaking announcement that may or may not affect literally anyone’s daily life, Microsoft proudly declared that its Edge browser is now 9% faster thanks to recent optimizations. According to Microsoft engineers, this performance boost is “noticeable,” though most users will likely only notice it for the three seconds they accidentally open Edge before downloading something else. Edge, which has spent the last few years rebranding itself from “that thing that launches when Chrome isn’t installed yet,” now boasts sleek Chromium-based architecture, vertical tabs, and a deep insecurity complex. With the new speed increase, Microsoft hopes people will finally stop treating it like the browser equivalent of a hotel Bible — always there, rarely opened. “We’ve worked tirelessly to improve Edge’s startup time, tab performance, and overall snappiness,” said a Microsoft spokesperson who probably uses Chrome. “We believe this 9% increase will truly enhance the experience for users who’ve somehow made it past the ‘default browser’ prompt without panic-clicking Chrome.exe.” The update arrives as part of Microsoft’s ongoing mission to convince the public that Edge isn’t just a Chrome costume with Bing stitched on the back. Despite its features like sleeping tabs and AI sidebar integration, users remain largely unconvinced.

“Oh cool, it’s faster,” said one user. “Now I can get to Google.com that much quicker so I can type ‘download Chrome.’” In related news, Internet Explorer’s ghost was spotted weeping softly in the recycle bin, whispering, “They never loved you either.”
