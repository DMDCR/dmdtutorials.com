Asus Routers hijacked in Stealthy Cyber Campaign

Over 9,000 ASUS routers have been silently hijacked in a stealth cyberattack that's probably building up to a massive botnet. This attack has been active since March 2025, but it was just made public on May 29.

Hackers used a known flaw:

CVE-2023-39780 — a command injection vulnerability. Plus, two authentication bypass tricks (still don’t have official CVEs).

Once in, they: Enabled SSH on a weird port: 53282, Added their own SSH key so they could come back later. Disabled logs and hid deep in memory that survives reboots/updates.

They kept the backdoor on all infected routers in NVRAM, used on startup. Allowing it to leave absolutely ZERO trace of being on the victim's router.
